# Player Guide — "Monopoly × Cashflow 101"

Welcome! You're a college student building a financial life. Your goal: **make your money work for you**
so your **passive income covers your monthly expenses** — that's "escaping the rat race," and the first
team to do it wins. Full rules live in [game-mechanics.md](game-mechanics.md); this is your quick guide.

---

## Your team

- You get a **financial profile** (Hustler, Med/Law Student, Scholarship Kid, Shopaholic, Wealthy Kid, or
  Influencer). Everyone starts with **$2,000 cash** and **~$1,000 net cashflow per payday** — but your
  income, expenses, and debts are different, so your strategy is too.
- Your **Financial Statement** (on the banker's screen) is your scoreboard: Income, Expenses, Assets,
  Liabilities, and your **Passive Income vs Monthly Expenses**. You read it; the banker updates it.

## A round = two phases

**1. Board phase — take your turn:**
1. **Roll two dice** and move that many spaces (clockwise).
2. **Resolve the space** you land on (see below).
3. Each time you **pass or land on START or an Extra Payday**, you collect your **net cashflow** (a payday).

While you move, the **stock market on the screen keeps moving and news breaks — but you CANNOT trade yet.**
Watch it. Feel the FOMO. Don't act.

**2. Trading Window (3 min):** the board pauses and prices freeze. **Now** you can buy/sell stocks & the
index, lock/redeem term deposits, pay down debt, or sell a business. Then the next round starts.

## What each space does

| Land on… | What happens |
|---|---|
| **Ownable Asset** (Online Shop, Campus Laundromat, Vending, Dorm Sublet, Parking, Food Stall, E-bike Rental Fleet) | **Buy it** ($1,000–$4,000) → it pays you passive income **every payday** (~30% of the price, so it pays for itself in ~3–4 paydays). If another team already owns it, **pay them rent** (half its income). |
| **Skill Up** (Online Course, Prof. Cert, Bootcamp, Marketing Course) | **Pay** → a **permanent** income bump every payday. A top return on the board — but it does **not** count toward escaping. |
| **Opportunity** | Draw a **Small or Big Deal** — buy a business for passive income (or pass). These are **illiquid**: you can only sell in a Trading Window, at **half price** (unless a buyer appears). |
| **Life Happens** | Draw from **your profile's** deck (2 lose money, 2 gain money, 1 free skill-up +$100/payday). |
| **Campus Life** | Draw a shared event card (gain or lose). |
| **Temptation** | A discretionary spend ($50–$300). Lifestyle creep! |
| **Fixed Bill / Tax** | Pay phone/rent/tax. |
| **Market News** | A **buyer** appears for a specific business — if you own it, sell at a **premium (~1.5×)**. If you don't, you can sell the card to the team that does. |
| **START / Extra Payday** | Collect your net cashflow. |

## The market (Trading Window only)

- **8 stocks + 1 S&P 500 index fund** on the screen, each based on a **real-world example** (tap **ℹ Stock
  Info** to see what each one is). Prices move on their own; you trade only in the window.
- Every trade (buy **or** sell) costs a **$50 fee** — so flipping constantly bleeds money.
- **Read the news.** During the Trading Window the screen shows two **🔮 Upcoming** lines — a hint that one
  stock will **rise** and another will **fall** next round. Use them to buy/sell *now*, before the move
  happens. When the round plays, the matching headline breaks (the news box **flashes red**) and the
  affected stock cards **blink** — green if rising, maroon if falling. Notice the balance: when one sector
  jumps, a rival sector usually dips — yet the S&P 500 keeps grinding up.
- **Dividends:** the 4 **stable** stocks (99 Speedmart, Tenaga, Maybank, Maxis) pay you **$5/share every
  payday** — a steady cashflow that *counts toward escaping the rat race* and keeps paying even when prices
  dip in the crash. A calm, beginner-friendly way to build passive income (the screen shows each one's
  yield %). Growth stocks, Dogecoin, and the S&P 500 pay no dividend.
- **Term Deposit:** lock **$1,000 → get $1,100 back** after 2 windows. Safe, low, never crashes.
- **Watch out:** the boring **S&P 500** quietly climbs; the hyped **Dogecoin** pumps then crashes. Chasing
  hype usually ends badly.

## How you win

- **Escape the rat race:** be the first team whose **Passive Income ≥ Monthly Expenses**. Passive income
  comes from **owned assets, deals, and stock dividends** — *not* salary, *not* skill bumps, and *not*
  stock price gains (those are only cash once you sell).
- **If nobody escapes by the end:** the team with the **highest Passive Income ÷ Expenses ratio** wins
  (tie → highest **Net Equity** = assets − liabilities).
- **No cash to pay?** You're forced into a **personal loan at 10%** — expensive. Keep a cash buffer.

## The 3 traps to catch yourself doing

1. **FOMO / hype** — buying Dogecoin because everyone is.
2. **Lifestyle creep** — every Temptation feels small; together they sink you.
3. **Over-trading** — flipping stocks for small gains while fees and the next crash eat you. Boring, steady
   investing (the Index) usually wins.

Good luck — build the asset column. 🎯
