Proposal to Organise an Event, “Finance Proofing 101”

The July ‘25 ALSCO Leadership Development (LD) Department would like to propose and seek approval to organise its termly free event, “Finance Proofing 101”, a personal finance themed talk and interactive workshop. The event aims to equip students with the knowledge and skills required to take charge of their financial futures. This event will be held on Thursday, 25th June 2026, from 5:15 p.m. to 8:40 p.m. within the Sunway College Campus and is open to all A-Level students. This event will involve 30 participants from the A-Level programme and will be offered free of charge to all participants.

The event begins with an opening ceremony and followed by a talk from the invited guest speaker covering core financial pillars: personal finance, behavioral finance, debt and cashflow management, and wealth accumulation. Following the talk, students will participate in a life-sized, interactive board game that combines mechanics of both Monopoly and Cashflow 101. Teams will take turns rolling the dice while their representatives navigate a physical board, landing on spaces simulating real estate, business acquisition, penalties, liabilities and payday. To maximise relatability, the game features teenager-specific expenses such as attending parties or buying birthday gifts which demonstrates the real-time impact of everyday financial habits.

Additionally, each team is assigned a unique financial profile such as the “Hustler”, the “Scholar”, the “Shopaholic” or a “Medical Student with High Debt”, each with distinct income levels, expenses, assets and liabilities. These realistic profiles challenge students to apply their newfound financial knowledge in real-life situations. 

To further simulate real-world economics, a dynamic stock market trading window opens after every full round of dice rolls. Market fluctuations force players to make quick, critical decisions under time pressure on whether to buy or sell. This teaches students on how to manage the “Fear of Missing Out” (FOMO) and underscores that consistency rather than beating the market is the key to successful investing. 

The event will conclude with a closing ceremony, with all participants receiving a keychain and the guest speaker receiving an engraved wooden plaque as a token of appreciation.

An Internal Trial Run (ITR) will be conducted on Xth June 2026, followed by an External Trial Run (ETR) on Xth June 2026 involving all event committees and ALSTAR volunteers. These trials are crucial for refining the logistics, activities, event flow and participant experience prior to the Actual Event (AE).

Risk Mitigation
Risk: There is a delay in the planned event timeline. 
Mitigation: The Event Coordinator will keep a close eye on the time and will adjust the schedule accordingly upon any delays. Any changes in the schedule will be informed to event committees for immediate schedule rearrangement and crowd control.

Risk: There is a noticeable lack of participant involvement and understanding during the game session. 
Mitigation: To foster engagement, the event committees will proactively guide participant interactions. Prior to each segment, comprehensive instructions and game mechanics will be briefed to ensure clarity regarding the upcoming activities. 

Risk: The target number of participants is not reached.
Mitigation: The publicity team and Event Coordinator will prepare a comprehensive range of promotional materials for posting on social media platforms.

Risk: Late participant arrivals. 
Mitigation: Keep participants informed by sending WhatsApp reminders before the event starts. Include the location and reporting time and plan for a strict 5-minute buffer time.

Risk: Technical issues or glitches during the event. 
Mitigation: Comprehensive pre-event testing of all projector screens, programs and media files will be conducted on Internal Trial Run (ITR) and External Trial Run (ETR). Any anomalies will be addressed immediately. 

Risk: No response from the external parties contacted.
Mitigation: A follow-up email will be sent out to each external party. If there is a further lack of response, alternate external parties that were previously shortlisted will be considered and contacted.
